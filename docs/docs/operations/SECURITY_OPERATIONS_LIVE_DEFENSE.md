# Rhelma Realm — Live, Collective, Adaptive Security System (Rhelma 6)

Security in Rhelma Realm is a **living, collective, adaptive** system.
It exists to protect:
- **Autonomy & Freedom** of citizens
- **Privacy & Data Residency** constraints
- **Ownership** of accounts, assets, and digital identity
- **System Continuity** ("alive under any condition")

The system is intentionally **anti‑capture**: no role, node, or individual can unilaterally enforce high‑impact actions.
It is built on distributed trust, healthy competition, and fair incentives.

---

## Article 1 — Core Principles

1. **Collective duty:** every citizen may contribute to defense by reporting threats and improving safety.
2. **Zero‑trust posture:** claims are not trusted without verification; trust is earned via behavior, reputation, and (when required) attestation.
3. **Proportionality:** responses must match threat severity and evidence strength.
4. **Defense without tyranny:** security must never become a mechanism for central control.
5. **Auditability by default:** actions are logged; sensitive details are minimized and access‑controlled, but integrity is verifiable.

---

## Article 2 — Three‑Layer Adaptive Defense

### Layer 1: Everyday Guardians (All Citizens)

- Any citizen can submit a report (including “one‑click report” flows).
- Reports are allowed without evidence, but become **low‑strength signals** unless evidence is provided.
- Verified reports earn **micro‑rewards** to incentivize broad participation.

**Tools:**
- One‑click report
- Simple evidence attachment (optional)
- Personal history of submitted reports and outcomes

### Layer 2: Honorary Police (Trained Volunteers)

- Citizens with positive history and required trust level may apply.
- A short mandatory in‑realm tutorial is required.
- Responsibilities:
  - higher quality reporting,
  - triage assistance,
  - incident response support,
  - education for new citizens.
- Tools:
  - personal monitoring dashboard,
  - anomaly alerts,
  - guided response playbooks.
- Rewards:
  - recurring Realm Credits,
  - verifiable badge.

**Power boundaries:**
Honorary Police can **recommend** and **escalate** actions, but cannot unilaterally execute irreversible enforcement.

### Layer 3: Digital Champions (Top Defenders)

- Selected through healthy competition based on verified impact:
  - major threat detection,
  - prevention of high‑impact incidents,
  - high‑quality policy proposals.
- Selection uses **automatic scoring + citizen voting** to prevent capture.
- Champion status is time‑limited (e.g., 6 months) and rotates by design.

**Powers (proposal‑only):**
- propose emergency policies,
- prioritized jury seat,
- priority review lanes for incidents.

All champion proposals remain **vetoable by citizens / jury** and bounded by governance rules.

---

## Article 3 — Execution, Safeguards, and Anti‑Abuse

### 3.1 Distributed detection pipeline
- Signals may come from citizens, volunteer defenders, automated observability/anomaly detection, and “guardian agents”.
- A **System Judge** (decision-support engine) performs initial analysis and generates a recommended plan.

**Rule:** the System Judge is a recommender, not the final authority.

### 3.2 Multi‑party response
High‑impact actions require multi‑layer confirmation:
- System Judge recommendation
- trained defender confirmation (Honorary Police or equivalent)
- **Human Jury** approval for irreversible or rights‑impacting actions

### 3.3 Evidence tiers (mandatory)
Define evidence tiers that determine allowed actions:

- **Tier 0 (weak signal):** no evidence → observation only
- **Tier 1 (soft evidence):** partial evidence → rate-limit / additional monitoring
- **Tier 2 (strong evidence):** strong evidence → temporary suspension pending review
- **Tier 3 (confirmed):** verified exploitation → enforcement + incident response

### 3.4 Anti‑abuse and appeals
- Deliberately false or malicious reporting triggers penalties:
  - reputation loss,
  - credit clawback,
  - rate limit reduction.
- An appeal path must exist to prevent harassment and false positives.
- Repeat false positives reduce signal strength automatically.

### 3.5 Mandatory security onboarding
- New citizens must complete a short security tutorial covering:
  - reporting norms,
  - privacy expectations,
  - common threat patterns,
  - “do not harass / do not spam” rules.

### 3.6 Guardian AI agents
- Specialized “guardian agents” may continuously scan for anomalies and propose mitigation.
- They must remain **human-in-the-loop** for irreversible actions.
- Guardian agents are auditable: decisions must reference evidence, traces, and policies.

### 3.7 Emergency defense fund
- A fixed treasury portion is reserved for major incidents and recovery operations.
- Fund usage is governed by multi‑party approval and audit logs.

---

## Article 4 — Rewards and Value

- **Layer 1:** micro‑rewards for verified contributions.
- **Layer 2:** recurring rewards + badge.
- **Layer 3:** special treasury allocation, bounded governance weight, and a “Hall of Heroes” record.

**Privacy note:** hall-of-heroes records may be redacted to protect citizen privacy while preserving integrity.

---

## Article 5 — Transparency, Privacy, and Justice

1. Security events are public at the **metadata level** (what happened, when, category, outcome).
2. Sensitive details remain access‑controlled, but integrity is verifiable via signed audit records.
3. Responses must be proportional, time‑bounded, and reviewable.
4. No permanent rights removal without human review and a defined appeal mechanism.

---

## Implementation mapping (Roadmap hooks)

- **Phase 4:** trust tiers + attestation + reputation thresholds (eligibility for Layer 2).
- **Phase 5:** jury + appeals + emergency fund mechanics + guardian agents v1.
- **Phase 6+:** decentralized evidence propagation and federation-aware incident response.
